Latest market sentiment (last 7 days, ~May 31–June 7 2026) is sharply risk-off after the strong May NFP print. High-engagement X discussion centers on a classic “good news is bad news” repricing.

**NFP Jobs Report (June 5 release)**
- +172k vs ~85k consensus — clear beat.
- Immediate reaction: Nasdaq -4.2% (worst day since April 2025), S&P -2.64%, Dow -1.35%. Tech/semiconductors led the selloff (Marvell -17%, Micron -13%, broad chip names down 7-11%).
- VIX spiked ~34-40% intraday. 10y yield rose ~6bp.
- Narrative split:
  - Headline-driven view: stronger jobs + sticky inflation = fewer/no Fed cuts, possible hike risks.
  - Contrarian thread (high likes): ~70k leisure/hospitality (World Cup bartenders) + 55k local gov made the number look hotter than trend. Core cyclical sectors still cooling. AHE cooled to 3.4% YoY. Many expect revisions later.

**Fed Rate Path**
- Markets rapidly repriced toward “higher for longer.” Focus now on June FOMC (new Chair Kevin Warsh, dot plot revisions). Some posts flag potential rate-hike language if inflation stays firm. Trump preference for lower rates noted but secondary to data.

**BOJ / JPY 160**
- USD/JPY back at the 160 level that previously triggered intervention.
- BOJ widely expected to hike 25bp (possibly larger) at the June 15-16 meeting. Inflation data (CPI 2.8%+, PPI 4.9%) cited as justification.
- Dominant theme: yen carry-trade unwind. Years of cheap JPY funding for US stocks/tech/crypto/EM now reversing, amplifying the broad risk-off move across assets. BOJ decision viewed as one of the highest-impact events for global markets in the near term.

**Gold Sell-off**
- Gold dropped ~3.2–4.5% around the NFP release to ~$4,331/oz (2026 low). Silver fell harder (7-9%).
- Drivers: stronger USD/yields + reduced appeal of non-yielding assets. Technical breakdown of 200-day MA and key supports.
- Sentiment: mostly treated as a leveraged correction after a long bull run rather than the end of the structural case (central bank buying still cited). Near-term support watched at 4300–4280.

**VIX Spike**
- Single-day surge of ~39–40% on June 5, highest since March ceasefire period.
- Coincided with equity flush and some Middle East geopolitical references.
- High-engagement takes: historical instances of ≥30% VIX spikes have generally been followed by positive 6–12 month S&P returns (small sample). Some view it as necessary vol expansion after extreme complacency. VIX:VXV ratio also spiked sharply.

**Overall Macro Sentiment**
- Risk-off whipsaw dominated. Strong NFP acted as catalyst to unwind crowded AI/tech positions and force repricing of rate expectations.
- Tone on X leans toward “violent but potentially healthy flush/positioning reset” rather than 2022-style regime change, though caution remains elevated into upcoming CPI, PPI, and dual FOMC/BOJ meetings.
- Carry-trade mechanics and VIX mechanics threads saw particularly high engagement.

Key near-term catalysts: June 10 CPI, FOMC (June 16-17), BOJ policy decision (June 15-16). Sentiment remains data-dependent and volatile.
